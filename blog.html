celebrated on 17.11.2021

Following an anti-Nazi demonstration, the regime sent 1200 students to concentration camps and closed all Czech universities. Since 1941, 17 November has symbolised the fight for freedom and democracy. The commemoration of this event was later used by students in 1989 to protest against the ruling Czechoslovakian Communist Party, leading to a violent confrontation with law enforcement. DIWALI CELEBRATIONS AT JNTUA

Celebrated on 4 november 2021

Diwali is a well-known festival in India. It is also known as the festival of lights that symbolizes diminishing darkness with the bright light. The foremost thing that hits the mind when you hear about Diwali will be of celebration with crackers, sweets, and gifts. Bursting crackers was one of the vital rituals of the celebration where it changed over in the modern world. In the meanwhile, people realized that crackers do promote noise and air pollution in a huge extreme. With all the environmental consciousness JNTUA Staff and Students started to celebrate diwali without crackers. Even without crackers, we proved the day can be zestful with other means.
